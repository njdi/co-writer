## 1. Idea + Compelling Storyline

To avoid overthinking ideas, you can use Claude

Create a separate chat and feed it these rules for generating storylines:

**1. Language Rules**
- Use some American slang + light profanity
- Never use the ban words
- No British slang: "bloody", "mate", "innit", "dodgy"
- Use slang sparingly — a couple of words per line, not everywhere
- Target audience — American

**2. Prompt Structure for Grok**
- `[VISUAL]` — location description + ALL characters in the frame
- `[DIALOGUE]` — numbered lines with speaker descriptions
- `[CAMERA]` — camera movement + mood

**3. Speaker Description Rules**

Each line must include:
- Character appearance
- Clothing
- Position in the frame
- Voice tone + accent + emotion
- Physical action after the line
- Never refer to characters by name

**4. Dialogue Rules**
- Natural rhythm — mix short and long exchanges
- Max 2 speaking characters per scene
- A third character can be present but silent
- Each line must be at least 10 words — no single-word lines

**5. Location Rule**
- Detailed description of each location at the end of the script
- All characters in the scene must be included in `[VISUAL]`
- Don't describe static elements — only what moves

**6. Camera**
- Always static
- Very slow, barely noticeable zoom
- No music

**7. Characters**
- Described only by appearance

Go through Instagram and TikTok in your niche, find high-performing videos, and feed them to Claude

Ask it to break down why they went viral and how they hook viewers within the first few seconds

## 2. Nano Banana + Character Creation

![Image](images/img-02.jpg)

Now you need to create your own universe

Don't design characters that are identical to those already existing in popular animated shows

If you do, you risk falling into the "high-risk" category for copyright strikes or even getting blocked

Instead, pick your favorite cartoon as a reference. Find images of its characters and upload them to Nano Banana. Then ask it to generate 5 original, random characters in a similar style, but not copied directly

After that, you can refine them however you want and request any changes you like

Finally, feed the result into Claude and tell it to remember that these are now the main characters of your new show

When you ask Claude to write a storyline, it will also give you the location where the scene takes place

It will look something like this:

![Image](images/img-03.jpg)

The prompt will also include the characters that should appear in the scene

All you need to do is attach them in the Nano Banana chat and paste the generated prompt

Next, take the generated image into Grok and paste in the full scene prompt along with the voiceover provided by Claude

Make sure to tell Claude that the scene should be no longer than 6 seconds or up to 10 seconds if you have SuperGrok

After completing all the steps, you should end up with something like this:

## 3. Setting Up Social Media Accounts

We will use 3 social networks:

- YouTube
- Instagram
- TikTok

For the first two, you just need to create accounts using Google accounts, let the account sit for a day, and for a couple of days scroll through the recommended feed and naturally interact with content similar to yours, until your entire feed becomes videos like yours

With TikTok, the situation is different

In addition to all the previous steps, you need to:

- Buy a US SIM card
- Buy a proxy with a US IP address
- Register the account
- And upload videos only while using all these conditions

## 4. Monetization

Most of your payouts will come from standard platform monetization

But Instagram doesn't have built-in monetization like TikTok or YouTube, so here you rely on collaborations with popular (and not so popular) brands, where you can take offers and integrate them into your videos

That's why it's important to target an adult audience, since this increases your chances of landing better deals — advertisers are more likely to invest when they know the content isn't aimed at kids

This income stream only opens up once you have consistent high views and a large number of followers

So this isn't a quick way to make money, as many people claim, it's a long-term approach where you invest effort and develop your skills over time

And don't expect millions of views right away, the algorithms need time to figure out who to show your videos to

(And for those who will say YouTube bans AI content — that's not true, the key is to avoid generic, template-style content, since you can lose monetization even with fully manually created content)
