# How to Sell AI Services When Nobody Knows Who You Are (7 Easy Methods)

**Author:** Corey Ganim ([@coreyganim](https://x.com/coreyganim))  
**Published:** Feb 25, 2026  
**Source:** [How to Sell AI Services When Nobody Knows Who You Are](https://x.com/Zephyr_hg/status/2026666463675765106)

Yesterday I posted about 4 AI side hustles paying $2,000–$12,000/month.

My DMs exploded.

The #1 question: "Okay, but how do I actually SELL these services?"

Fair. Knowing what to sell is useless if you can't find clients.

Here are 7 ways to land your first AI clients. No fancy marketing budget required. Just effort.

---

## 1) Host an AI For Business Meetup in Your City

This is the highest-leverage play nobody's doing.

Here's why it works: Business owners are curious about AI but overwhelmed. They don't know who to trust.

When you host the meetup, you're automatically positioned as the expert.

I hosted one last month. I walked away with 7 qualified leads. One of them became a client within a week.

**How to do it:**

- Find a free venue (library, coworking space, coffee shop with a back room)
- Create a simple Eventbrite or Lu.ma page
- Post in local Facebook groups and LinkedIn
- Keep the format simple: 20-minute presentation on "3 Ways Local Businesses Are Using AI Right Now" followed by Q&A
- Collect emails/phone numbers at the door

You don't need to be the world's leading AI expert. You just need to know more than the people in the room.

And that bar is much lower than you think.

## 2) Door Knock Local Businesses

Old school? Yes. Effective? Absolutely.

Walk into 10 local businesses this week. Dentists, real estate offices, HVAC companies, law firms. Say this:

> "Hey, I help local businesses save 10+ hours a week using AI tools. I'm offering a free 15-minute audit to show you what's possible. Interested?"

Most will say no. Some will be curious. One or two will say yes.

That's all you need.

The business owners who say yes are self-selecting. They're already interested in AI but don't know where to start. You just made it easy for them.

Do this for 30 days. You'll have more leads than you can handle.

## 3) LinkedIn Outreach (Done Right)

Cold DMs work when they're not salesy garbage.

**Target:** Business owners with 10–50 employees in your city. They're big enough to have real problems but small enough that the owner still makes decisions.

**Message template:**

> "Hey [Name], I noticed you run [Company] here in [City]. I've been helping local businesses automate repetitive tasks with AI. Not trying to sell you anything, just curious: what's the most annoying part of your day-to-day operations?"

That's it. Start a conversation. Don't pitch in the first message.

Send 20 of these per day. Within a week, you'll have 5–10 conversations going.

## 4) Offer Free "AI Audits" to Your Network

You already know business owners. Friends, family, former coworkers, people from your gym or church.

Text them this:

> "Hey, I've been learning how to help businesses save time with AI. Would you be open to a 20-minute call where I look at your workflow and tell you what could be automated? No charge, I'm just building my portfolio."

Free work? Yes. But here's what happens:

- You get real practice
- They get real value
- They tell their friends
- Word of mouth kicks in

My first three paying clients came from doing free audits.

One of them referred me to two more.

After you have a few case studies you can charge for the audits (I now charge $999 for a 45-minute audit).

## 5) Partner with Agencies and Consultants

Other service providers already have the clients you want.

Marketing agencies, business coaches, accountants, web developers. They work with business owners every day. Many of them are getting asked about AI and have no idea what to say.

Reach out with this:

> "Hey, I specialize in AI automation for small businesses. If any of your clients ever ask about AI, I'd love to be your go-to referral. Happy to give you a cut of any deals that come through."

Now you have a sales team without hiring anyone.

## 6) Create "AI Office Hours" at a Coworking Space

Find a local coworking space. Offer to host free weekly "AI Office Hours" for their members.

You show up for 2 hours every Thursday. Members drop in with questions. You help them solve problems on the spot.

**What you get:**

- Credibility (you're the resident AI expert)
- Warm leads (people who already trust you)
- Content ideas (every question is a potential post or article)

Most coworking spaces will say yes because it adds value for their members. You're not asking for money. You're offering to help for free.

The paying clients come naturally.

## 7) Post Your Wins on Social Media (Consistently)

Every time you help someone, document it.

"Just helped a local dentist automate their appointment reminders. Took 45 minutes to set up. Now it runs on autopilot."

"Built an AI assistant for a real estate agent that drafts listing descriptions in 30 seconds. Used to take her 20 minutes each."

You don't need a huge following. You need consistency.

Post 3–5 times per week for 90 days. Mix client wins with educational content. People in your network will start reaching out.

The goal isn't to go viral. The goal is to stay top of mind so when someone thinks "I need help with AI," they think of you.

---

## The Bottom Line

You don't need a marketing budget. You don't need a big audience. You don't need to be a tech genius.

You need to show up, offer value, and make it easy for people to say yes.

Pick one method from this list. Do it consistently for 30 days. Then add another.

The AI services market is wide open. The only question is whether you'll go get it.
